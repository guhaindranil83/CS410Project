Adaptive Instruction Methods in Computer Science, Th 5:00PM - 6:59PM, Internet/Online Biography Christopher Hunn earned a Bachelor's in Cognitive Science (Neuroscience Track) from UC Berkeley, a Biological Sciences Education credential from Cal State East Bay, and has done doctoral-level coursework in Policy Organization Measurement and Evaluation at Berkeley. He started work in the EECS department at UC Berkeley in 2012, and worked his way up from Graduate Student Advisor to CS Advisor and Undergraduate Affairs Supervisor, then to Director of Undergraduate Affairs in Computer Science, and finally to EECS Director of Undergraduate Instruction in 2017. Before coming to work at Berkeley, Hunn spent five years at the West Contra Costa Unified School District from 2005 to 2009, and was an instructor and curriculum developer at NASA Ames Research Center from 2000 to 2002.
