She received her B.S. degree in marketing, management, and entrepreneurship from the University of WisconsinMadison in 2013. After her graduation, she spent time as a project manager for a construction company, a paraprofessional educator, and a research assistant for a professor at Georgia State University. Azizas experience as a research assistant helped her discover a passion for consumer research, prompting her to apply to Rutgers University. After joining Rutgers in 2015, she began studying how social factors influence consumer behavior. Her dissertation investigates how consumers use products to influence how they are perceived by others, and how social identities impact consumer behavior. In addition to progressing her research, she has served on the planning committee for the PhD Project Marketing Doctoral Student Association and as a junior reviewer for the Journal of Consumer Research. Apart from academics, Aziza, enjoys snowboarding, running, and playing chess.
