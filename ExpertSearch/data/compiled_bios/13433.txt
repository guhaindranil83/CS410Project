Brown Assistant Professor (ME, MSE, Physics) Office Photonics, Rm 920 Email brownka@bu.edu Phone (617) 353-4841 Website Brown Research Group Assistant Professor (ME, MSE, Physics) Primary Appointment Department of Mechanical Engineering Education Ph.D. Harvard University Additional Affiliations Division of Materials Science & Engineering Physics Department Honors and Awards Deans Catalyst Award, 2018 Member, Materials Research Society, 2017 Moorman-Simon Interdisciplinary Career Development Professor, 2016 MSE Innovation Award, 2016 Classes Taught ENG ME 303 Fluid Mechanics ENG ME 306 Introduction to Materials Areas of Interest Top-down patterning and bottom-up assembly Mesoscale soft materials Scanning probe techniques Soft matter-electric field interactions Research Areas The Brown group is an interdisciplinary research program at the intersection of nanotechnology and soft materials with three goals: (1) Learn how to make novel materials by merging the strengths of top-down patterning and bottom-up assembly. (2) Investigate how mesoscopic order affects the behavior of soft materials such as polymers and proteins. (3) Apply these lessons to make new materials and devices that leverage hierarchical structure.
