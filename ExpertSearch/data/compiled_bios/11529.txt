Biography Biography Narges Shayesteh Moghaddam is an Assistant Professor in the department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at the University of Texas at Arlington. Before joining UTA in 2018, she received her B.S. in mechanical engineering from Isfahan University of Technology and her M.Sc. and Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Toledo. Dr. Shayesteh Moghaddams research interests lie in the development, production, and characterization of novel materials for applications in automotive, oil and gas, and biomedical devices. Of particular focus is the area of additive manufacturing, or 3D printing, of topology-optimized functional structures. This work encompasses a wide range of research activities including, but not limited to, the following: functional materials science, design for additive manufacturing, materials processing, materials property characterization, exploring the link between microstructure and mechanical properties, structural/failure analysis/monitoring, laser melting process optimization, topology optimization, and post-processing treatments. Another particular focus is the area of design, development and fabrication of patient-specific porous implants made of shape memory alloys.
