Audience Tools Search Menu Faculty > David Levy David Levy CV Professor dmlevy@uw.edu Mary Gates Hall 330T Phone: 206-616-2545 | Fax: 206-616-3152 Website Specializations Contemplative Practices Ethics of Information and Technology Information and the Quality of Life Research Areas Information and Society Health and Well-Being Courses LIS 552 - Critical And Existential Perspectives On The Information Society Biography David Levy holds a PhD from Stanford University in computer science (1979) and a Diploma in calligraphy and bookbinding from the Roehampton Institute, London (1982). For fifteen years (until December, 1999), he was a member of the Xerox Palo Alto Research Center (PARC) where his research focused on the nature of documents and on the tools and practices through which they are created and used. His current research focuses on information and the quality of life. He is the author of "Scrolling Forward: Making Sense of Documents in the Digital Age" (Arcade, 2001).
