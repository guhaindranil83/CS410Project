He received his Ph.D. in Applied Physics from Yale University in 1991, after which he worked as a research scientist for Philips Research Laboratories (The Netherlands), and for Hitachi Research Laboratory, based at the University of Cambridge, England. There he was presented with the Hitachi Manager Director's award for his research on spin electronic devices and circuitry. He joined the University of Louisville in 2000, where his recent research focus includes energy scavenging and high efficiency photovoltaic devices. He has published over 110 peer reviewed journal articles, holds 10 US and European patents, and has been invited to speak on his work at numerous international symposia. Since joining the University of Louisville, he has been PI or co-PI on over $45 M in research funding.
