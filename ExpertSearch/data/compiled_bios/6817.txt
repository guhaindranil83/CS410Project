The primary methods used in his group are quantum mechanics-based density functional theory and regression/classification techniques based on neural networks, ridge regression and random forests. His research deals with large materials datasets relevant for solar cells and related optoelectronics, and using state-of-the-art data science approaches to create useful models and online design tools. Education Ph.D. Materials Science and Engineering, University of Connecticut, 2017. Bachelor of Technology, Metallurgical and Materials Engineering, 2012. Employment History Assistant Professor, School of Materials Engineering, Purdue University, 2020-Present. Postdoctoral Associate, Center for Nanoscale Materials, Argonne National Laboratory, 2017-2020.
