His interests lie in the philosophy of mathematics and its history, in philosophy of logic, and in mathematical logic. His written work is currently focused upon neologicism and the philosophy of mathematical practice. He has recently published Infini, logique, gomtrie (Vrin, Paris, 2015) (Prix Jean Cavaills 2018), Abstraction and Infinity (Oxford University Press, 2017), and Moscow Has Ears Everywhere. New Investigations on Pasternak and Ivinskaya (Hoover Press, Stanford, 2019).
