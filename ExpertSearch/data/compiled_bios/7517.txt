Jackson Professor, Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Following his graduate studies at MIT in 1985, Patrick Jaillet held successive positions as a tenured faculty in applied mathematics at the cole Nationale des Ponts et Chausses (ENPC) in Paris from 1985 to 1991, and as a professor in management science at the University of Texas at Austin from 1991 to 2002. He came back to MIT as professor and head of Civil and Environmental Engineering in 2002, joined LIDS and EECS in September 2009, and became the Dugald C. Jackson Professor in July 2010. He is a fellow of the Institute for Operations Research and Management Science (INFORMS) and a member of the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM), and served as associate editor for the INFORMS journal Operations Research from 1994 until 2005. He is also an associate editor for the scholarly journals Networks, Transportation Science, and Naval Research Logistics.
