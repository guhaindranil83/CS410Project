To achieve this mission, my group aims to provide design principles for energy-related materials based on understanding of how their structure and chemistry together determine the properties. It is our strength that we carry out highly interdisciplinary research at the intersection of materials science, inorganic chemistry, applied electrochemistry, and chemical engineering. Our research topics include the development of electrochemical materials for energy storage, analytic methods for micro-scale impedance spectroscopy, and tools for in situ structural characterization. We also address issues in scalable processing of inorganic solid electrolytes for low-cost all-solid-state batteries.
