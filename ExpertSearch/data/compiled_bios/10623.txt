Hariri Building 595 Commonwealth Avenue Boston, MA 02215 Download CV Gerdus Benade is an assistant professor in the Information Systems department. His research considers questions at the intersection of computer science and economics, like making group decisions under uncertainty and the fair division of resources in dynamic settings. He is particularly interested in applications of optimization and computational social choice to traditionally political issues like voting, participatory budgeting and political districting or gerrymandering.
